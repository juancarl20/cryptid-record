Mothman Is Simply Running Late

A reputation for prophecy, built entirely on a scheduling problem.

Mothman has spent decades branded a harbinger, a creature whose appearance foretells calamity. The Institute's position is duller and, we think, kinder: he is a commuter with a long route and a chronic punctuality problem, and the disasters are a matter of when he arrives, not why.

The timing, examined

Every notable appearance precedes a calamity by a short margin. Read as prophecy, this is uncanny. Read as someone arriving flustered just as a situation turns, it is an ordinary bad evening. His route is long. He leaves with good intentions. He arrives, every time, at the exact moment things are already going sideways, which is the worst possible moment to be seen looking alarmed.

He is not warning you. He got here as fast as he could, and he can see it is already going badly.

On the glowing eyes

Witnesses fixate on the red eyes and read menace into them. The Institute reads exertion. Anyone who has sprinted the last stretch of a long commute arrives wide-eyed and breathing hard. The glow is, at most, the look of a creature that ran to get here and still missed it.

If you see him, the guidance is to skip the questions. Do not ask what is coming. He does not know either. He only just walked in, and he is as surprised as you are.
