The Nain Rouge Just Has Bad Timing

Blamed for every disaster it happens to attend. The Institute finds correlation, not causation.

The Nain Rouge, the red dwarf of Detroit, is treated as a harbinger — its appearance is said to precede the city's misfortunes. The Institute, reviewing the long pattern, finds a creature with catastrophic timing and a habit of turning up uninvited, and no evidence that it causes anything at all.

The pattern

The Nain Rouge attends events. Some of those events go badly, as some events always will. Because it is small, red, and impossible to forget, it is the detail every witness retains, and so the misfortune gets pinned to its presence rather than to any of the dozen ordinary reasons things were already going to go wrong.

It does not bring the disaster. It arrives, uninvited, and the disaster was already on the calendar.

The reputation

Centuries of this have left the Nain Rouge with a reputation it cannot fix by behaving well, because the problem was never its behavior. It simply keeps appearing right before things it had nothing whatever to do with. The Institute considers it among the most unfairly filed entries in the catalog, and has said so, repeatedly, to no measurable effect on the legend.
