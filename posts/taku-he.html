Taku-He Is Overdressed

Reliably reported in a top hat and long coat. Not a disguise — simply the best-dressed entry in the catalog.

Taku-He, the tall figure of South Dakota, is consistently described in a top hat and a long dark coat. The Institute does not read this as a disguise, or an omen, or anything sinister. It reads a wardrobe, and an unusually formal one.

The wardrobe

A top hat and a long coat are specific, deliberate choices. Taku-He makes them every single time, in all weather, regardless of the occasion — and the occasion is rarely formal. The result is a creature perpetually overdressed for its surroundings: a tall figure in eveningwear, standing alone in an open field at dawn, dressed for somewhere it is plainly not.

It is not dressed for the moment. It is dressed for a better moment that has so far not arrived.

The standard

Across the entire catalog, Taku-He is the best-dressed entry by a comfortable margin. The Institute notes this without judgment. A creature that maintains a coat and a top hat in the open country of South Dakota, with no audience and no event to attend, has standards — and standards, on their own, have never been grounds for suspicion.
