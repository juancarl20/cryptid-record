The Hodag Is Still Embarrassed About the Horns

A fearsome reputation from a single bad first impression, and a hundred years of trying to live it down.

Rhinelander's Hodag is drawn as a horned terror, all spines and teeth. The Institute's encounters describe something else entirely — a gentle creature, mortified by its own appearance, that has spent a century quietly hoping the reputation will fade.

The first impression

The original sighting went badly. The Hodag, startled and loud, made an impression that hardened into legend before it had any chance to explain itself. Everything since has been an attempt to correct a record that was set, permanently, in a single bad afternoon.

The horns were not a threat. They were just the first thing anyone happened to see.

The temperament

Reviewed across decades, the Hodag is shy, accommodating, and quick to apologize for startling people who, as a rule, startled it first. It is aware of how it looks. It would change it if that were possible. Since it is not, the Hodag keeps to the back of the woods and does its best not to loom, which is a great deal to ask of a creature built, through no fault of its own, entirely to loom.
