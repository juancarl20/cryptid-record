The Chupacabra Is Lactose Intolerant

The temper, the dairy-farm avoidance, and the bad press all trace to one digestive issue.

The chupacabra's reputation rests on livestock attacks it has spent years denying. The Institute's finding reframes the entire file. The creature is lactose intolerant, and very nearly all of its behavior follows from that single fact.

The temper

Chronic discomfort makes anyone short-tempered. The famous foul mood is not malice. It is the disposition of a creature that has, once again, made a poor dietary decision and is now living with the consequences in real time.

The dairy farms

Far from raiding them, the chupacabra gives dairy operations a wide and deliberate berth. Its proximity in the old reports is coincidence. It was passing through, looking unwell, and got blamed by people who already had a creature in mind to blame.

It is not stalking the herd. It is staying as far from the milk as the terrain will allow.

The livestock reputation, then, is a case of mistaken identity compounded by worse press. The Institute does not expect to correct the record in its lifetime, but it has, at least, filed the correction.
