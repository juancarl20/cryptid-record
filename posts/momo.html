Momo's Smell Is Cologne

The odor that precedes the Missouri Monster is applied on purpose and, by its own estimation, excellent.

Momo, the Missouri Monster, is famous for arriving smell-first. Witnesses describe the odor as a warning, a stench, a thing to flee. The Institute describes it as a choice — cologne, deliberately applied, of which Momo is openly and sincerely proud.

The application

The smell precedes Momo by a considerable distance, which the creature regards not as a problem but as the mark of a strong and confident scent. It applies generously. It reapplies. It believes, without a flicker of doubt, that it smells wonderful, and approaches each encounter quietly expecting to be complimented on it.

It is not a stench. To Momo, it is a signature, and the signature is bold.

The Institute's position

We have elected not to be the body that corrects Momo on this. The creature is large, the creature is confident, and the creature has plainly never once received honest feedback on the scent. There are files worth reopening and files worth leaving exactly as they are. This is firmly the latter.
