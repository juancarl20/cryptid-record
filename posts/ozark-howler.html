The Ozark Howler Was Going to Start a Podcast

The howls are vocal warm-ups. The show never launched. The creature still has notes.

The Howler's long, mournful calls are read as menace, or grief, or a warning to stay out of the hollows. The Institute reads vocal exercises — specifically, warm-ups for a podcast that reached the planning stage and stopped there.

The format

The proposed show was to cover regional folklore from the inside: a cryptid interviewing cryptids, with the access that only a fellow legend could secure. The Howler had a name picked out, a rough release schedule, and what it described to itself as a strong first season. What it did not have was funding.

It is not howling at the moon. It is running the exercise it would have run before recording.

The notes

Funding fell through late in the second season of planning, which is the worst possible time for funding to fall through. The Howler, understandably, still has notes — on the format, on the timing, and on the several parties who said they would help and then, when it mattered, went quiet.

Filed by the Standards Board · Pilot unrecorded · Funding withdrawn, season two
