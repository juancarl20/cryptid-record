About the Institute

A field-research body of long standing, sound methods, and no physical location.

The Midwest Institute for Cryptid Studies maintains a working catalog of facts about creatures that have not been proven to exist, concerning behaviors they have not been proven to have. We file these straight-faced because the straight face is the whole point. A folded slice of cheese is funnier when it arrives stamped, numbered, and cross-referenced.

Our method

Each entry receives a catalog number, a status, and a plausibility rating between one and ten. The ratings are assigned by the Standards Board, which consists of whoever is in the room. None of the ratings mean anything. That is itself a finding.

The fine print, said plainly

None of this is real. The Institute is invented. The cryptids are folklore, the facts are made up, and the field notes are comedy written in the costume of a research bulletin. Nothing here is a claim about the actual world, and nothing here should be cited as one. If you arrived hoping for evidence that the Honey Creek Monster dislikes small talk, we are delighted you came, and we have to be honest with you.

The real folklore underneath — the Van Meter Visitor, the Iowa Thunderbird, the regional cryptid record — is genuine and worth reading about from genuine sources. Everything we have added to it is ours, and it is fiction.

Submissions

Have a creature and a deadpan fact to match it? That is the entire format. The best entries take a real piece of cryptid lore and supply the boring, human, weirdly specific reason behind it. Keep the face straight. The cheese stays folded.
