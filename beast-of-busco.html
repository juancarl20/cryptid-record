The Beast of Bray Road Is Protecting Its Form

An upright runner, on a fixed evening route, that would rather not break stride for traffic.

Wisconsin's Beast is reported loping upright along a rural road at dusk, a sight drivers find threatening. The Institute finds something less dramatic: a creature mid-run, on a route it keeps, that would prefer not to break stride for passing cars.

The form

Running upright is a choice, not a charge. The Beast maintains good posture, a steady cadence, and a route that happens to follow Bray Road for a stretch. Drivers catch it at speed, in low light, and read intent into what is, from its side of things, simply the evening's workout being interrupted by headlights.

It is not chasing the car. The car is on its road, during its run, at its hour.

The schedule

Sightings cluster at dusk because dusk is when it runs. Earlier and it is too warm. Later and the light is gone. The window is narrow and consistent, which is why the same stretch of road produces the same startled report, year after year, from people who happened to drive through someone else's exercise hour.
