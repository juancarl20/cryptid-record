Minutes from the Lake Monsters' Union

Collective bargaining beneath the surface, since 1971, and the treasurer who keeps the books.

In 1971, after a century of unpaid appearances and a tourism economy built entirely on their likenesses, the world's lake monsters organized. The Institute holds a partial copy of the founding agreement. It explains a great deal that had previously been filed under mystery.

What the contract covers

Visibility hours are capped. Blurry-photograph residuals are owed whenever an image is sold. Tourism boards that profit from a monster's name pay into a shared fund. The famous reluctance to be photographed clearly is not shyness at all. It is the enforcement of a clause, and a well-drafted one.

No clear photographs without prior written agreement. The blur is contractual.

The treasurer

Nessie holds the regional treasurer post and is, by every account, exacting. The books balance. Champ and Ogopogo serve on the board. The position rotates in theory, but Nessie keeps being re-elected, partly on competence and partly because no one else is willing to do the spreadsheets. If your photograph came out blurry, it is because you had not signed anything.
