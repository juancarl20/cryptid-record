The Michigan Dogman Keeps a Seven-Year Planner

The long gaps in the record are administrative, not mystical. The creature simply keeps a planner.

The Dogman is famous for appearing in years ending in seven. The supernatural reading treats this as a curse, or a cycle, or a pattern in the fabric of things. The Institute reads a planner — a seven-year one — that the creature refuses, on principle, to update.

The cycle

It is not bound to the seventh year by any force. It is bound by its own calendar, purchased once, laid out in seven-year blocks, and treated as fixed law. When the planner says it is time, it appears. When the planner is silent, so is it. The mystery dissolves the moment you accept that the creature is simply organized.

The gaps are not a mystery. The gaps are the years the planner left blank.

The principle

Asked, notionally, why it does not just buy a new planner, the Dogman's position is stubbornness dressed as method. The system works. The system has always worked. The system requires it to appear roughly once a decade and otherwise be left entirely alone — which, the Institute suspects, is rather the point of keeping it.
