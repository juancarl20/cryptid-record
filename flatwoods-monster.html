The Third Leg Is a Kickstand

Witnesses cannot agree on the leg count because the count genuinely changes.

The 1973 Enfield, Illinois sightings describe a creature with three legs, or with two, depending entirely on which witness you ask. The Institute resolves a fifty-year dispute simply: the third leg is a kickstand, lowered at rest and retracted in motion.

The mechanism

When the creature stops, a third support comes down for stability. When it moves, the support folds away. A witness who sees it standing counts three. A witness who sees it moving counts two. Both are correct, which is exactly why the file has been an unresolved mess since the year it opened.

The leg is real when it is needed and gone when it is not. This is what a kickstand is.

The hops

The Enfield Horror is also reported to move in great bounds, which the Institute finds entirely consistent. A creature with a retractable third leg would naturally favor a gait that gets it off the ground, where the whole question of how many legs it is standing on becomes, briefly and conveniently, no one's business.
