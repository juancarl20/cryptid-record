CR-0007Subject: JackalopeStatus: FolklorePlausibility 1/10

The Antlers Are for Hats

No defensive purpose whatsoever. A courtship conducted entirely through millinery.

Naturalists assume the antlers are weapons, or display, or some leftover of an older lineage. The Institute, reviewing spring behavior, finds they exist to hold small hats, and that hat selection is the whole of jackalope courtship.

The season

Each spring the males assemble and cycle through their hats. A well-chosen brim signals taste, restraint, and judgment. A poor one ends the pairing before it begins. There is no fighting, no display of strength, no contest of speed. There is only the hat, and the silent verdict rendered upon it.

A bad hat has cost more promising matches than any predator on the plains.

The record

The Institute has catalogued forty-one hats across three seasons. The most successful, by a clear margin, was a modest felt number in dark green, worn by a male who otherwise had little to recommend him. The least successful was a novelty hat that its wearer believed was funny. It was not, and he knew it by sundown.
