The Melon Heads Are Undefeated at Trivia

Encyclopedic recall, a flawless competitive record, and not one game they were ever allowed to play.

Michigan's Melon Heads are described with fear, almost entirely on account of the heads. The Institute, looking figuratively inside them, finds an encyclopedic command of regional history and a long-thwarted ambition to compete at pub trivia.

The advantage

The recall is total. Dates, names, the year the courthouse burned, the middle name of every county commissioner since 1880 — the Melon Heads hold all of it, effortlessly, at all times. At a trivia table they would be unbeatable, and every establishment that has ever turned them away knows it perfectly well.

Their record is undefeated. Their record is also empty. Both facts have exactly the same cause.

The ban

No establishment has permitted them to enter. The heads are alarming, the recall is suspected of being some kind of cheat, and the Melon Heads have been quietly declined at every quiz night they have ever approached. They remain, on a technicality, undefeated. They would trade the entire record, gladly, for a single permitted game.
